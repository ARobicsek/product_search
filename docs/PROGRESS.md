**Finding worth remembering:** ADR-028 introduced a deliberate seam — "LLM writes one qualitative paragraph, everything else deterministic" — as a *partial* enforcement of ADR-001's no-fabricated-data invariant. The post-check existed because the LLM had ONE remaining job and could still misbehave inside it. With ADR-096 the seam is closed: every byte of the report surface is now produced from verified data. The post-check, the `PostCheckError` retry loop, the `synth_v1.txt` prompt — all become dead-code scaffolding kept only for backwards-compat imports and possible re-introduction. The general pattern: when a "necessary evil" architectural concession (an LLM doing qualitative prose) finds itself no longer load-bearing (the user doesn't read it), the right move isn't to polish it — it's to retire the seam entirely and reclaim the structural guarantee. Tracking what's load-bearing vs. inherited-from-an-earlier-decision is worth doing periodically; it's how dead-code accretes silently.
